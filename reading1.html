Question 1: What if a good working defintion of what a System is and perhaps what it is not?

A system is a group of elements or parts and their interactions/relationship with each other A system is not a singular object with no relations.

Question 2: For the 4 system definitions you were to look up please give in your own words what they mean and give an example of them for each case.

Modularity: A system having the ability to adapt and have different parts to continue functioning. There is flexability in how system can be organized. An example of this is Legos.

Decomposability: The breaking down of a system into fundamental parts. An example of this is a Matroshyka doll.

Emergence: The micro level of a system functioning towards a greater or larger goal. An exmaple of this is gears, construction, or electronics(circuit boards).

Chaos Theory: The non linear or seemingly random output from initial inputs. An example of this is climate change or survival of the fittest.

Question 3: What is the difference between ideas of Modularity and Tesselation? What are the properties that are in oppostion with each other?

The main differences are to do with the rigidity and uniformity of the system. Tesselations are rigid and highly repeatable. Modularity are flexible and highly adaptable.

Question 4: What is the difference between designing something that has Complexity (aperiodic) vs being Uniform (periodic)?

The difference resides in the process. Complex design typically has varying results while uniform have the same pattern, resulting in the same outcome.

Question 5: What is the difference between a system diagram and a some kind of feature diagram like a like a exploded view drawing or a illustration like this lady bug.

Exploded view drawing explains the parts of an object rather than the system and purpose A system diagram gives information about each part of a system and their interactions. They also revolve background the conceptual nature of an ecosystem as opposed to the specific details of each part.
